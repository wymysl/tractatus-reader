## Explanation

The "1" branch is closed. 1.2 opens a new one, and it sounds, at first,
like a plain restatement of 1.1: "the world divides into facts" is close
kin to "the world is the totality of facts, not of things." But a
restatement wouldn't earn its own number in this book — 1.2 is doing
something 1.1 didn't.

1.1 was a claim about what the world is *made of*: facts, not things.
1.2 is a claim about *shape*. "Divides into" (*zerfällt in*) says the
facts are not a fused, seamless block that merely happens to be called
"the world" — the world comes apart, cleanly, into separate pieces. Each
fact is a fact on its own, not a smear that only counts as many facts
because we choose to describe it that way. Take away one fact and the
others are not disturbed; that is what it means for something to
*divide*, rather than merely to be describable in parts.

This sounds almost too small to be worth a sentence, but it is doing
preparatory work for what is coming. 1.13 named logical space and left
it unbuilt. 1.2 is the first brick: if the world divides cleanly into
facts, then each fact must occupy its own place in that space,
independently of the others. The next thesis, 1.21, will say exactly
that — any one fact can hold or fail to hold while everything else stays
exactly as it is. 1.2 is what makes 1.21 possible to state at all.

## Method

New branch, so a fresh check: does 1.2 strain the tree view or the
glossary tripwire? No new technical term — "divides into facts" leans on
"fact," already explained at 1.1, not a new pair. No change to `site/`
or `build.mjs`; the tree page already shows a new top-level child of "1"
opening beside the closed 1.1 branch, same mechanism as every day so far.

Held 1.2 against the doubt test for a Zen note and found nothing honest
to say. The Zen-adjacent move would be something like "the world
'divides' the way phenomena arise as distinct, dependently" — but that
is exactly backwards: dependent origination denies that phenomena are
freestanding once divided, while 1.2's whole point is that each fact
*is* freestanding once the world divides. That is closer to a real
disanalogy than a connection, but forcing one for its own sake, on a
thesis this thin, would be filler dressed as rigor. Omitting the section
entirely — silence, per METHOD.md's own instruction, is the more honest
outcome here than a stretched disanalogy just to have kept the streak
from days 4 and 5 going.
